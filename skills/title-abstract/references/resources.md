## Key Research Findings

### On Title Length
- Optimal: 10-12 words (traditional recommendation)
- Average in mathematics: 12.3 words
- Maximum effective: ~16 words
- Longer titles (>20 words) fare poorly in peer review

### On Title Type
- Declarative titles receive more citations
- Avoid interrogative titles in mathematics
- Specific titles outperform vague ones

### On Abstract Structure
- Lead with contribution, not background
- State results quantitatively
- 150-250 words typical for papers
- Must stand alone (readers often see only abstract)

### On Keywords
- 5-15 keywords correlates with higher citations
- Use synonyms across title/abstract/keywords
- Standard terminology improves discoverability

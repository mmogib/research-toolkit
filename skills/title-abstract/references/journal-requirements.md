# Journal-Specific Requirements

## SIAM Journals

**Applies to:** SIAM Journal on Optimization, SIAM Journal on Numerical Analysis, SIAM Journal on Scientific Computing, SIAM Review, etc.

### Title
- Brief and appropriate for indexing
- Specifically describe the content
- Running head: max 50 characters (including spaces), no abbreviations

### Abstract
- One paragraph, max 250 words
- Summarize principal techniques and conclusions
- Relate to known results
- Minimize mathematical formulas
- Minimize bibliographic references (write out in full if essential, not by number)
- LaTeX commands specific to article should be avoided

### Keywords
- Required
- Include MSC (Mathematics Subject Classification) codes

### Reference
- [SIAM Author Instructions](https://epubs.siam.org/journal-authors)

---

## Springer Journals

**Applies to:** Mathematical Programming, Computational Optimization and Applications, Journal of Optimization Theory and Applications, Computational and Applied Mathematics, etc.

### Title
- Concise and informative
- Avoid abbreviations and formulae where possible

### Abstract
- 150-250 words
- No undefined abbreviations
- No unspecified references
- Should stand alone

### Keywords
- 4-6 keywords for indexing

### Reference
- [Springer Submission Guidelines](https://link.springer.com/journal/40314/submission-guidelines)

---

## Elsevier Journals

**Applies to:** Journal of Computational and Applied Mathematics (JCAM), Applied Numerical Mathematics, Linear Algebra and its Applications, etc.

### Title
- Concise and informative
- Avoid abbreviations where possible

### Abstract
- Max 250 words typically
- Concise and factual
- State purpose, principal results, major conclusions
- No references, no undefined abbreviations

### Keywords
- Immediately after abstract
- Max 6 keywords

### Reference
- [JCAM Author Guide](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-computational-and-applied-mathematics/publish/guide-for-authors)

---

## AMS Journals

**Applies to:** Mathematics of Computation, Transactions of the AMS, Proceedings of the AMS, etc.

### Title
- Appropriately reflect content
- Follow AMS style conventions

### Abstract
- Required for all articles
- Concise summary
- Follow AMS Style Guide

### MSC Codes
- Required (2020 Mathematics Subject Classification)

### Reference
- [AMS Author Resource Center](https://www.ams.org/publications/authors)
- [AMS Style Guide](https://www.ams.org/publications/authors/AMS-StyleGuide-online.pdf)

## Mathematics Subject Classification (MSC)

### Common Codes for Computational/Applied Math

**Primary:**
- 65-XX: Numerical analysis
- 90-XX: Operations research, mathematical programming
- 49-XX: Calculus of variations and optimal control

**Specific:**
- 65K05: Numerical mathematical programming methods
- 65K10: Numerical optimization and variational techniques
- 90C25: Convex programming
- 90C29: Multi-objective and goal programming
- 90C30: Nonlinear programming
- 49M37: Numerical methods based on nonlinear programming
- 52A27: Approximation by convex sets

### How to Choose MSC Codes
1. Search [MathSciNet MSC lookup](https://mathscinet.ams.org/msc/msc2020.html)
2. Choose 1 primary code (most representative)
3. Choose 1-3 secondary codes
4. Use 2020 MSC classification (current standard)

## Pre-Submission Checklist by Venue

### For SIAM
- [ ] Abstract ≤ 250 words
- [ ] Running head ≤ 50 characters
- [ ] MSC codes included
- [ ] No abbreviations in running head
- [ ] Minimal math formulas in abstract
- [ ] References in abstract written out (not numbered)

### For Springer
- [ ] Abstract 150-250 words
- [ ] 4-6 keywords provided
- [ ] Abstract stands alone
- [ ] No undefined abbreviations

### For Elsevier
- [ ] Abstract ≤ 250 words
- [ ] ≤ 6 keywords
- [ ] No references in abstract
- [ ] No undefined abbreviations

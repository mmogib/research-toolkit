# Document-Specific Guidelines

## MS Thesis

### Title Characteristics
- Length: 12-18 words acceptable (slightly longer than papers)
- Should indicate scope and methodology
- May include "A Study of..." if appropriate for thesis format
- Check department guidelines for format requirements

### Title Patterns
- "[Method/Analysis] of [Problem] with Applications to [Domain]"
- "[Technique] for [Problem]: Theory and Computation"
- "Numerical Methods for [Problem Class]"

### Abstract Characteristics
- Length: 150-300 words (check department requirements)
- May be structured with headings (Introduction, Methods, Results, Conclusions)
- Should be accessible to committee members from related areas
- Include: problem statement, approach, main results, significance

### MS Abstract Template
```
[Problem statement and motivation - 2-3 sentences]

[Approach/methodology - 2-3 sentences]

[Main results - 3-4 sentences]

[Conclusions and significance - 1-2 sentences]
```

## PhD Thesis/Dissertation

### Title Characteristics
- Length: 12-20 words acceptable
- Should reflect the breadth of the dissertation
- May encompass multiple related contributions
- Balance: specific enough to be meaningful, broad enough to cover scope

### Title Patterns
- "[Broad Topic]: [Specific Focus] and [Related Focus]"
- "Contributions to [Field]: [Aspect 1], [Aspect 2], and [Aspect 3]"
- "[Methodology] for [Problem Class] with Applications"
- "Theory and Algorithms for [Problem Domain]"

### Abstract Characteristics
- Length: 300-500 words (check department requirements)
- Often structured with clear sections
- Must convey the unified thesis/argument
- Should mention all major contributions (typically 3-5)
- Include broader impacts and significance

### PhD Abstract Template
```
[Opening: Central problem/theme of dissertation - 2-3 sentences]

[Background and gap - 2-3 sentences]

[Contribution 1 - 2-3 sentences]

[Contribution 2 - 2-3 sentences]

[Contribution 3 - 2-3 sentences]

[Validation/applications - 1-2 sentences]

[Broader significance and future directions - 2-3 sentences]
```

### PhD vs MS Differences
| Aspect | MS Thesis | PhD Dissertation |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| Scope | Single focused contribution | Multiple related contributions |
| Novelty | May be incremental | Must be original/significant |
| Abstract length | 150-300 words | 300-500 words |
| Title length | 12-18 words | 12-20 words |
| Contribution count | 1-2 main results | 3-5 main results |

## Grant Proposals

### Title Characteristics
- Length: Often strictly limited (e.g., 81 characters for NSF)
- Must be compelling and accessible to panel
- Avoid jargon that limits understanding
- Emphasize impact and innovation

### Title Strategies
- Lead with benefit/outcome when possible
- Use action words: "Advancing...", "Enabling...", "Developing..."
- Make the significance clear
- Accessible to interdisciplinary panel

### Grant Title Patterns
- "[Action]: [Specific Technical Goal] for [Broader Impact]"
- "[Innovation] for [Application Domain]"
- "Advancing [Field] through [Methodology]"

### Abstract Characteristics (NSF-style)
- Often 1 page or specific word limit
- Structured format typically required
- Must address: intellectual merit AND broader impacts
- Written for program officers and panelists (not just experts)

### NSF Project Summary Structure
NSF requires three sections:

1. **Overview** (1 paragraph)
   - What is the project?
   - What are the goals?
   - What approach will be used?

2. **Intellectual Merit** (1 paragraph)
   - What is the potential to advance knowledge?
   - Why is this approach innovative?
   - What are the expected outcomes?

3. **Broader Impacts** (1 paragraph)
   - Benefit to society?
   - Broadening participation?
   - Educational components?
   - Dissemination plans?

### Grant Writing Tips
- Use active voice
- Be specific about outcomes
- Quantify when possible
- Connect to agency priorities
- Avoid excessive technical jargon
- Make significance clear to non-specialist

## Conference Papers/Extended Abstracts

### Title Characteristics
- Same guidelines as research papers
- May emphasize novelty/contribution more strongly
- Consider visibility in conference program

### Abstract Characteristics
- Usually shorter: 100-200 words
- Very focused on main contribution
- May omit detailed results for space
- Should attract readers to your talk/poster

### Conference Abstract Template
```
[Main contribution - 1-2 sentences]

[Key technical insight or method - 1-2 sentences]

[Main result - 1-2 sentences]

[Significance or application - 1 sentence]
```

# Title Guidelines for Mathematical Writing

## Optimal Length

- **Target**: 10-16 words
- **Mathematics average**: 12-14 words
- **Maximum recommended**: 20 words (longer titles fare poorly in peer review)
- **First 65 characters**: Include primary keywords (Google Scholar indexing)

## Title Types

### Declarative (Recommended)
State the main finding or contribution directly.
- "Optimal Convergence Rates for Proximal Gradient Methods under Strong Convexity"
- "Adaptive Metrics for Norm-Minimization-Based Outer Approximation in Convex Vector Optimization"

### Descriptive
Describe what the paper addresses without stating conclusions.
- "Convergence Analysis of Proximal Gradient Methods"
- "A Study of Adaptive Metrics in Vector Optimization"

### Interrogative (Use Sparingly)
Pose a question. Less effective for citations in mathematics.
- "Can Adaptive Metrics Improve Convergence Rates?"

**Recommendation**: Prefer declarative titles in mathematics. They communicate value immediately.

## Structure Patterns

### Pattern 1: Method + Application
"[Method/Technique] for [Problem/Application]"
- "Proximal Algorithms for Composite Optimization"
- "Interior Point Methods for Semidefinite Programming"

### Pattern 2: Result + Context
"[Key Result] in/for [Problem Domain]"
- "Superlinear Convergence in Quasi-Newton Methods"
- "Optimal Rates for Convex Optimization under Noise"

### Pattern 3: Two-Part with Colon
"[Broad Topic]: [Specific Contribution]"
- "Convex Vector Optimization: An Adaptive Metric Approach"
- "Proximal Methods: Sharp Convergence Bounds"

### Pattern 4: Contribution Type + Topic
"[Improved/Optimal/Sharp/New] [Object] for [Problem]"
- "Improved Convergence Rates for Stochastic Gradient Descent"
- "Sharp Bounds for the Proximal Operator"

## Keyword Placement

### High-Value Keywords for Computational/Applied Math
Place these prominently (first 65 characters when possible):

**Problem types**: optimization, programming, equations, systems
**Methods**: algorithm, method, approximation, iteration
**Properties**: convergence, rate, bound, complexity, stability
**Qualifiers**: optimal, adaptive, efficient, fast, parallel

### Field-Specific Terms
- Convex optimization: convex, proximal, gradient, dual, primal
- Numerical analysis: finite element, discretization, error, mesh
- Applied math: model, simulation, inverse problem, regularization

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Too Vague
- Bad: "A Study of Optimization Methods"
- Good: "Accelerated Gradient Methods for Strongly Convex Optimization"

### 2. Starting with Weak Phrases
Avoid: "A Study of...", "On the...", "Some Results on...", "A Note on..."
- Bad: "On the Convergence of Gradient Descent"
- Good: "Linear Convergence of Gradient Descent under Strong Convexity"

### 3. Too Narrow (Hurts Discoverability)
- Bad: "Convergence of Algorithm 3.2 in Smith (2019)"
- Good: "Convergence of Modified Proximal Point Methods"

### 4. Too Long
- Bad: "An Improved Analysis of the Convergence Rate of the Proximal Gradient Method for Solving Convex Optimization Problems with Lipschitz Continuous Gradients" (24 words)
- Good: "Improved Convergence Rates for Proximal Gradient Methods" (7 words)

### 5. Undefined Acronyms
- Bad: "Fast ADMM for LASSO Problems"
- Better: "Fast Alternating Direction Methods for Sparse Regression"
- Acceptable: "Fast ADMM for Sparse Optimization" (ADMM is well-known)

### 6. Missing Method or Problem
Title should indicate both what you do and what problem you address.
- Bad: "Adaptive Metrics" (missing problem)
- Bad: "Convex Vector Optimization" (missing contribution)
- Good: "Adaptive Metrics for Convex Vector Optimization"

## Searchability Checklist

1. Would someone searching for this topic find it?
2. Are primary keywords in the first 65 characters?
3. Does it use standard terminology (check MathSciNet, zbMATH)?
4. Would it appear in relevant Google Scholar alerts?
5. Can readers understand the topic without reading the abstract?

## Title Revision Process

1. **Draft** 3-5 candidate titles
2. **Check length** (aim for 10-14 words)
3. **Verify keywords** in first 65 characters
4. **Eliminate** vague/weak phrasing
5. **Test searchability** mentally
6. **Get feedback** from colleague outside immediate area

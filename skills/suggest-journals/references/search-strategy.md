# Search Strategy Reference

How to search effectively for journal candidates.

## 1. Scimago SJR

Scimago Journal & Country Rank is the primary source for quartile and SJR data.

**Search patterns:**
- Browse by subject area and category to find all ranked journals in a field
- Use the Scimago website search to look up specific journal names
- Filter by quartile (Q1, Q2) and country/region if needed

**Key data points to extract:**
- SJR indicator (Scimago Journal Rank)
- Quartile (Q1–Q4) — note the subject category, as a journal may be Q1 in one category and Q2 in another
- H-index
- Total documents and citations

**Web search queries:**
- `site:scimagojr.com "journal name"` — find a specific journal's Scimago page
- `scimago sjr [field] Q1 Q2 journals` — find ranked journals in a field
- `scimago [subject category] journal ranking` — browse category rankings

## 2. Publisher Journal Finders

Major publishers offer journal suggestion tools that match paper abstracts to journals.

**Elsevier Journal Finder:**
- Input: title, abstract, keywords
- Output: ranked list of Elsevier journals with scope match scores
- Search: `elsevier journal finder`

**Springer Journal Suggester:**
- Input: manuscript title and abstract
- Output: Springer Nature journal suggestions
- Search: `springer journal suggester`

**Wiley Journal Finder:**
- Input: title and abstract
- Output: Wiley journal suggestions
- Search: `wiley journal finder`

**IEEE Publication Recommender:**
- Input: manuscript title and abstract
- Output: IEEE journal/conference suggestions
- Search: `ieee publication recommender`

Note: These tools only suggest their own publisher's journals. Use multiple finders for broader coverage.

## 3. Community Sources for Response Times and Experiences

Self-reported review experiences are valuable for estimating response times and understanding editorial quality.

**Web search queries for response times:**
- `"[journal name]" review time experience reddit`
- `"[journal name]" time to first decision`
- `"[journal name]" review process site:reddit.com`
- `"[journal name]" review experience site:researchgate.net`
- `"[journal name]" editorial response site:academia.stackexchange.com`

**Dedicated review time databases:**
- SciRev (scirev.org) — researcher-reported review times
- Search: `site:scirev.org "[journal name]"`

## 4. Cross-Checking Indexing

### Web of Science (ISI) Indexing
- Search: `"[journal name]" web of science indexed`
- Master Journal List: search `web of science master journal list` to find the lookup tool
- Journals in WoS have an Impact Factor (JCR)

### Scopus Indexing
- Search: `"[journal name]" scopus indexed`
- Scopus Source List: search `scopus source list` to find the lookup tool
- Journals in Scopus have CiteScore

### Quick verification query:
- `"[journal name]" ISSN indexed scopus "web of science"`

## 5. Red Flags to Watch For

When evaluating journals, flag these warning signs:

- **Predatory indicators**: listed on Beall's List or successors, aggressive email solicitation, no visible editorial board, guaranteed fast acceptance
- **Excessive APCs**: article processing charges significantly above field norms without clear justification
- **No peer review transparency**: no information about review process on the journal website
- **Very new journals**: less than 3 years old with no established metrics — may be legitimate but carry risk
- **Misleading names**: journal names designed to be confused with established journals
- **Editorial board quality**: check that board members are active researchers in the field

## 6. Search Workflow

For a given paper topic, execute searches in this order:

1. **Scimago category search** — identify the 2–3 most relevant Scimago categories for the paper's topic. List all Q1–Q2 journals in those categories.
2. **Keyword filtering** — from the Scimago results, identify journals whose scope matches the paper's keywords.
3. **Publisher finders** — use 2–3 publisher journal finders with the paper's abstract/keywords for additional candidates.
4. **Community validation** — for the top 15–20 candidates, search for author experiences regarding review times and editorial quality.
5. **Indexing verification** — confirm ISI and Scopus indexing for each candidate.
6. **Final filtering** — apply user preferences (publisher exclusions, access model, response time threshold) to produce the final ranked list.
